How to Bridge to Scroll

To bridge to Scroll, connect a Web3 wallet like MetaMask, choose Ethereum as the source network, select Scroll as the destination, enter the token and amount, confirm gas fees, and approve the transaction. Funds typically arrive on Scroll Layer 2 within minutes, depending on network congestion.

Scroll operates as a zkEVM Layer 2 compatible with Ethereum smart contracts, allowing standard wallet connections and ETH transfers through its official bridge at https://scroll.io/bridge.

Follow these steps exactly. Do not rush confirmations. A wrong network selection can delay your transfer.

Step 1: Connect Wallet

Open the native Scroll bridge or a supported third-party bridge. Connect MetaMask or another EVM wallet and ensure Ethereum mainnet is selected.

Step 2: Select Token & Amount

Choose ETH or supported tokens like USDC. Enter the amount and review estimated gas fees before proceeding.

Step 3: Confirm Transaction

Approve the transaction in your wallet. Wait for Ethereum confirmation, then monitor the deposit on Scroll mainnet.

Is Bridge to Scroll Safe?

Bridge to Scroll is generally safe when using the official Scroll bridge or reputable third-party providers, but all cross-chain bridges carry smart contract and counterparty risk. Users reduce exposure by verifying URLs, sending test transactions first, and avoiding unknown bridge interfaces.

  • Smart contract risk exists — Bridges rely on audited contracts, yet exploits have historically occurred across the industry, including high-profile bridge hacks documented by blockchain security firms.
  • Official Scroll bridge alignment — The canonical bridge is developed alongside the Scroll zkEVM protocol, reducing dependency on external liquidity providers.
  • Non-custodial design — You retain wallet control at all times; no account registration or custody transfer is required.
  • Phishing is the real threat — Most user losses stem from fake websites or malicious approvals, not protocol failure.

Risk never drops to zero. Smart contract systems are immutable once deployed, and vulnerabilities can surface unexpectedly.
